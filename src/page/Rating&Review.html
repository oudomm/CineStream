4/5

How great it is to see the original cast of the series back, including Charlie Cox, Elden Henson, Deborah Ann Woll, Vincent D'Onofrio, Jon Bernthal, Wilson Bethel, and Ayelet Zurer. One thing I want to highlight is that Matt Murdock and Wilson Fisk had already appeared in other recent Marvel series, but they had lost the essence of their characters. This series brings that essence back, and as a fan of the Netflix version, I'm truly grateful - even though their characters were heavily affected by their appearances in other shows.

Absolutely phenomenal! The writing, acting, and direction are all top-notch. This is exactly what fans have been waiting for. The fight choreography is incredible and stays true to the original series. I particularly loved how they handled the character development for Foggy and Karen, giving them meaningful arcs that complement Matt's journey. David Thompson
3/5

It's good to see the original cast back, but the pacing feels off in the first few episodes. The villain's motivations aren't as compelling as in the original series, and some of the side plots feel unnecessary. That said, the action sequences are still excellent, and Charlie Cox continues to nail the role of Matt Murdock. I'm hoping the second half of the season picks up the pace and delivers on the promise of the original. The show has potential but needs to focus more on what made the Netflix series great.

Jennifer Lee
4/5

Vincent D'Onofrio's portrayal of Wilson Fisk is once again the highlight of the show. His menacing presence and complex character development make him one of the best villains in the Marvel universe. The show does an excellent job of exploring the moral gray areas that made the original series so compelling. I particularly enjoyed the legal drama aspects and how they balance the superhero elements. The only reason I'm not giving it 5 stars is that some episodes in the middle feel a bit stretched out. Overall, a worthy continuation of the Netflix series.
